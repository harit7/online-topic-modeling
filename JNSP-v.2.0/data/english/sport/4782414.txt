Young GB swimmers in record form

  Britain's young disabled swimmers were in record-breaking form at the National Junior Swimming Championships.  
 Rachael Latham, 16, knocked over half a second off the world record in the 100m fly (S8) at the weekend. 
 Claire Warne, 14, clocked two minutes 10.88 seconds in the (SM5) 100m individual medley to take nearly 16 seconds off the world record. 
 Meanwhile, Claire Cashmore, 17, came away with the European record in a time of 1.06.86 in the 100m freestyle (S9).  
 The competition, organised by Disability Sport Events, was held at Ponds Forge in Sheffield and welcomed 230 disabled swimmers between 10-18 years old.
