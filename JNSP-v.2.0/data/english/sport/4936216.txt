Belgium reach Fed Cup last four

        FED CUP SCORES      Spain 5-0 Austria   Germany 2-3 United States   France 1-4 Italy   Belgium 3-2 Russia        
   Justine Henin-Hardenne sent Belgium into the Fed Cup semi-finals with victory over Russia's Elena Dementieva.  
 Henin-Hardenne's 6-2 6-0 win on Sunday gave Belgium an unbeatable 3-1 lead before the defending champions pulled one back in the dead doubles rubber. 
 Earlier, world number two Kim Clijsters crushed Maria Kirilenko 6-1 6-4 before Henin-Hardenne set up a July showdown with the USA, who beat Germany 3-2. 
 Spain and Italy are in the other semi-final after winning their ties.  
  Spain  booked their place in the last four for the third successive year with a 5-0 whitewash against  Austria  . 
 Anabel Medina Garrigues helped the Spaniards to an unassailable 3-0 lead when she brushed aside Sybille Bammer 6-0 6-3 in the first match on Sunday. 
 Maria Sanchez Lorenzo edged out Austrian youngster Tamira Paszek 6-4 6-7 7-5 to put Spain four up before Virginia Ruano Pascual and Lourdes Dominguez-Lino wrapped up a 6-4 6-2 victory in the doubles rubber. 
 Their semi-final opponents,  Italy  , had a tougher time against  France  . 
 Francesca Schiavone broke the previous day's 1-1 deadlock by overcoming top-ranked Amelie Mauresmo 4-6 7-6 (4) 6-4, winning on a contested line call at match point. 
 Flavia Pennetta then gave Italy an unbeatable 3-1 lead, downing Nathalie Dechy 6-4 6-2 in the second reverse singles match, before a final victory in their remaining doubles match. 
 In  Germany  , Anna-Lena Groenefeld beat  USA's  Jill Craybas 6-2 7-5 to cut the home side's deficit to 2-1 following Saturday's rubbers. 
 But Jamea Jackson secured victory for the Americans with a 7-6 6-2 defeat of Martina Mueller, a late replacement for Julia Schruff, who had cramps during her loss to Craybas on Saturday. 
 Germany produced a late fightback in the doubles rubber for a consolation win as the USA went through.
